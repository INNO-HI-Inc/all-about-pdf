    Convert vector SVG files like logos and icons into PNG images to post straight to blogs, presentations, and messengers. Turn up the scale and they come out crisp instead of pixelated, and you can save with the transparent background kept or a white one — your choice. Your files never touch a server — everything is processed right in your browser (on your device), so you can use it with complete peace of mind.

    How to use

    SVG to PNG Converter How to use

    1. Drag and drop your SVG files, or browse to select them. You can convert several at once.
    2. Set the output scale (1x–4x). The bigger it is, the sharper and larger the PNG.
    3. Choose a transparent or white background.
    4. Click Convert to download your PNG. Multiple files come bundled in a ZIP.
    Your files are never uploaded to any server.Every conversion happens entirely inside your browser (on your device), and everything disappears the moment you close the tab. No file is ever sent to a server, so you can safely convert even sensitive SVGs like company logos or unreleased designs.
    Note

    When this comes in handy

    It's handy when you're dropping a logo SVG exported from a design tool (Figma, Illustrator) into a presentation, adding an icon to a messenger or Notion, or pulling a web vector into a high-resolution PNG for print. Just raise the scale and you can make everything from a small thumbnail to a large banner from the same SVG, without pixelation.

    Frequently asked questions

    Frequently asked questions

    Q1Why convert SVG to PNG?
    SVG may not display in places that don't support vectors, like messengers, blogs, and some document editors. Convert it to PNG and you can paste it like any image anywhere — and keep the transparent background intact, too.
    Q2Won't it get pixelated?
    SVG is a vector, so it won't pixelate even as you scale it up. Raise the output scale to 2x–4x and you get a crisp PNG that holds up on big screens and in print. For small uses, 1x–2x is plenty.
    Q3Can it save with a transparent background?
    Yes. Leave the background set to 'Transparent' and you get a PNG that keeps the SVG's transparent areas intact. If you need a white background, choose 'White'.
    Q4Can I convert several files at once?
    Yes — drag in multiple SVGs and convert them all in one go. A single image downloads right away, while multiple images come bundled in a ZIP file.
    Q5Are there SVGs that won't convert?
    SVGs that pull in external fonts or images from the internet may show those parts blank, since our policy is to never send your file outside your device. For fonts, use an SVG where the text has been converted to outlines (paths) ahead of time, and it will come out exactly as intended.
